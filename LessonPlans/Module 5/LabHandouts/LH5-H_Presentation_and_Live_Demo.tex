\labheader{5-H}{Final Presentation, Live Demonstration, and Capstone Submission}

\section{Objective}
Prepare and deliver the 20-minute capstone presentation (plus 10 minutes of Q\&A) that communicates the system architecture, experimental results, and lessons learned; include a live demonstration of real-time classification; and submit the complete curriculum package. This is the final handout of the capstone --- all prior deliverables feed this one.

\section{Prerequisites}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item Lab Handout~5-G complete (final report written and reviewed).
	\item Lab Handout~5-F complete (curriculum package assembled and verified).
	\item All hardware operational; inference pipeline runs end-to-end without manual intervention.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Step 2 --- Prepare each module slide}
The five module-highlight slides are the hardest to get right. For each one:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item Pick the \emph{single} most compelling figure or table from that module.
	\item State in one phrase (on the slide) the key design decision: e.g., ``32-B packet format / fixed fields'', ``8-feature vector at 100\,MHz'', ``5 sweep campaigns, stratified split''.
	\item State the key result in a sentence or a number.
	\item \emph{In your script}: end with a sentence that transitions to the next module (``this packet format is what Module~2 will parse'').
\end{itemize}

The transition sentences are the spine of the presentation --- they are what makes five modules feel like one project instead of five.

\subsection{Step 3 --- Live demo preparation}
The live demonstration is the most impactful part of the presentation and also the most failure-prone. Prepare four redundancies:

\begin{tcolorbox}[warning={Live Demo Redundancy Checklist}]
	\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.2em]
		\item \textbf{Pre-recorded backup video.} Record a 2--3 minute screen capture of the inference script running successfully, including the parameter-change moment. If anything fails live (\mbox{USB} port not recognised, beacon battery dead, bitstream corrupted), switch to the video without apology and without pausing the presentation.
		\item \textbf{Pre-test in the presentation room.} \mbox{RF} propagation in the presentation space differs from the lab. Run the system for 5 minutes in the actual room before your slot.
		\item \textbf{Minimise configuration changes.} Changing one beacon's SF live is impressive; changing multiple parameters simultaneously risks confusion. Pick one change, rehearse it, and stick to it.
		\item \textbf{Start the system during the architecture slides.} The system should be up and running by the time you reach the demo slide. The ``live'' portion of the demo is you switching to the already-running terminal, not you starting something cold.
	\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{tcolorbox}[warning={Rollback Trigger Criteria --- Switch to Backup Video If}]
	\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em]
		\item \textbf{No classification output for $>$20\,s} after reaching the demo slide (system has stalled).
		\item \textbf{Two consecutive \mbox{CRC} rejects or parser rejects} visible in the terminal (link corruption that will not self-heal during the demo).
		\item \textbf{Reconfigured beacon fails to update within 15\,s} of the parameter change (beacon firmware crashed or USB reset dropped).
		\item \textbf{Any \texttt{Traceback} in the inference terminal} (Python-side failure; do not try to recover on stage).
		\item \textbf{Projector or terminal text not visible from the back row} (presentation readability is a hard rollback trigger even if the system is healthy).
	\end{itemize}
	If any trigger fires: say one short line (``Let me cut to the recorded run for time''), switch to the backup video, and continue. Do not debug on stage.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Step 4 --- Demo script}
Write and rehearse a verbatim script for the live demo:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item \emph{``On screen is the live output of our inference script. Each line represents one packet classified on three independent tasks.''}
	\item \emph{``You can see Beacon~1 and Beacon~2 are both currently classified as SF7, LoRa, with the correct beacon \mbox{ID}s.''}
	\item (Reconfigure Beacon~1 to SF10; wait for the next packet.)
	\item \emph{``I've just reconfigured Beacon~1 to SF10. Watch Beacon~1's classification switch on the next packet --- there --- while Beacon~2 remains at SF7.''}
	\item \emph{``The total latency from \mbox{TX} start to this line appearing is the number on the right: about 55 milliseconds.''}
	\item (Return Beacon~1 to SF7 to end on a clean configuration.)
\end{enumerate}

Total demo runtime, including narration, should be 2--3 minutes. Any longer and the audience's attention budget for the remaining slides suffers.

\subsection{Step 5 --- Rehearsal}
Two full rehearsals, ideally on different days:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item \textbf{First rehearsal --- timing.} Present to an empty room or to one peer. Measure slide-by-slide time. If any slide takes more than 90 seconds, cut content. If the total exceeds 20 minutes, cut slides.
	\item \textbf{Second rehearsal --- questions.} Present to a peer or advisor who has not seen the project details. Ask them to interrupt with questions. Note every question --- some will appear again in the actual \mbox{Q\&A} session.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Step 6 --- Anticipate Q\&A topics}
The 10-minute \mbox{Q\&A} is often where the final impression is made. Prepare one-sentence answers to at least the following likely questions:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item Why did you choose a random forest (or whichever model won)? Cite the LH4-H rubric.
	\item What is the sim-to-real gap and why?
	\item If you had to pick one thing to redo, what would it be? Use the LH5-G Discussion answer.
	\item What is the largest cross-domain dependency you found? Cite the LH5-E catalog.
	\item How would this scale to 10 beacons? 100?
	\item What is the \FPGA{} resource utilization? Cite Module~2 final utilization report.
	\item Why did you use VS Code with the STMicro extension stack rather than standalone STM32CubeIDE for firmware? Cite the Module~1 reference environment choice.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Step 7 --- Submit the complete package}
Package submission is the last act of the project. Check:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item Final report (from LH5-G) in both PDF and source form.
	\item Presentation slides (PDF + original source).
	\item Backup demo video.
	\item Complete curriculum package (from LH5-F).
	\item All repositories tagged with a final release version.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Step 8 --- Deliver the presentation}
On the day of the presentation:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item Arrive 30 minutes early. Test the projector connection. Test audio if the backup video has sound.
	\item Start the beacons and inference script during your architecture slides (Step~3 redundancy \#4).
	\item Do not rush transitions. A 2-second silence between slides reads as confidence, not hesitation.
	\item During \mbox{Q\&A}, if a question stumps you, say ``I don't know --- that would be an interesting follow-up experiment''. Do not invent.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[infobox={The Closing Message}]
	Every presentation about an engineering project is implicitly answering one question: ``What did you learn?'' The instinct is to answer with what was built --- the beacons, the \FPGA{} pipeline, the classifier. The better answer is to describe a \emph{cross-module} insight: a case where a decision in one domain constrained or enabled something unexpected in another. That is the answer the framework is designed to produce. End on that insight.
\end{tcolorbox}
