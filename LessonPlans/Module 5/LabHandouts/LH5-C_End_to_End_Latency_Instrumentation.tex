\labheader{5-C}{End-to-End Latency Instrumentation and Per-Stage Decomposition}

\section{Objective}
Instrument the full pipeline --- beacon \mbox{TX} start $\to$ host classification output --- with a GPIO-toggle latency measurement and produce a per-stage decomposition of the total response time. This handout extends the LH3-D \mbox{GPIO}-toggle method from a single stage (\mbox{RF} + firmware) to all six stages, giving the Module~5 benchmarking table in LH5-D a physical measurement per stage rather than a theoretical budget.

\section{Prerequisites}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item LH3-D complete (\mbox{GPIO}-toggle method validated for beacon$\to$\FPGA{} latency).
	\item Two-channel oscilloscope or logic analyzer available.
	\item USB-\mbox{GPIO} adapter (e.g., Arduino, FT232H, or Raspberry Pi Pico) for the host-side toggle. If unavailable, an alternative using \texttt{frame\_valid} as the endpoint is documented in Step~5.
\end{itemize}

\section{Latency Model}
\[
	T_{\text{total}} = T_{\text{RF}} + T_{\text{RX\_FW}} + T_{\text{UART\_in}} + T_{\text{FPGA}} + T_{\text{UART\_out}} + T_{\text{infer}}
\]

\begin{table}[h!]
	\centering
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
	\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l c l X}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Symbol} & \textbf{Method} & \textbf{Description} \\
		\midrule
		\mbox{RF} on-air          & $T_{\text{RF}}$       & Calculated from SF/BW & LoRa packet duration on the air \\
		Receiver firmware          & $T_{\text{RX\_FW}}$    & \mbox{GPIO} toggle     & \mbox{ISR} + frame construction in STM32 \\
		\mbox{UART} to \FPGA{}     & $T_{\text{UART\_in}}$  & Calculated            & $43 \times 10 / 115{,}200 = 3.73$\,ms \\
		\FPGA{} pipeline           & $T_{\text{FPGA}}$      & \mbox{ILA}            & UART-RX-complete to \texttt{frame\_valid} \\
		\mbox{UART} to host        & $T_{\text{UART\_out}}$ & Calculated            & $35 \times 10 / 115{,}200 = 3.04$\,ms \\
		Host inference             & $T_{\text{infer}}$     & \texttt{perf\_counter()} & Parse + scale + predict \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\section{Procedure}

\subsection{Step 1 --- Beacon-side GPIO toggle}
From LH3-D, Beacon~1 already toggles PA8 (or the board \mbox{LED} on PA5) just before \texttt{sx1276\_send()}. Confirm this is still present in the firmware. If not, re-add the single-line \texttt{HAL\_GPIO\_WritePin} immediately before the \mbox{SPI} transmit that initiates the \mbox{TX}.

\subsection{Step 2 --- Host-side GPIO toggle}
Wire a USB-\mbox{GPIO} adapter to the host. In \texttt{live\_inference.py}, add a one-line pin-toggle call on every classification print:

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Host-side toggle snippet --- add just before the \texttt{print()} in the live loop.}]
# gpio is a handle to a USB-GPIO adapter (Arduino-Firmata, FT232H, pyftdi...)
gpio.toggle(HOST_LATENCY_PIN)   # 1 us resolution via FT232H
print(f"[{ts}] SF={preds['sf']['label']} ...")
\end{lstlisting}

The toggle should fire on the \emph{first line} of the output path, not inside the \texttt{print()} call, so that Python stdout buffering does not inflate $T_{\text{infer}}$.

\subsection{Step 3 --- Scope capture}
Connect:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item Ch.~1: beacon \mbox{GPIO} toggle (rising edge marks \mbox{TX} start).
	\item Ch.~2: host \mbox{GPIO} toggle (rising edge marks classification output).
	\item Ch.~3 (optional, if a 4-channel scope is available): \FPGA{} \texttt{frame\_valid} routed to \mbox{PMOD} JA[2] (as in LH3-D).
\end{itemize}

Trigger on Ch.~1 rising. Capture at least 50 consecutive events per operating point.

\subsection{Step 4 --- Per-stage decomposition}
Combine measurements and calculations per operating point:

\begin{table}[h!]
	\centering
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
	\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l X l}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Measurement} & \textbf{How to derive} & \textbf{Required uncertainty} \\
		\midrule
		$T_{\text{total}}$        & Ch.~2 rising edge $-$ Ch.~1 rising edge (scope cursor) & $\pm$(scope cursor resolution + trigger jitter) \\
		$T_{\text{RF}}$           & Theoretical LoRa on-air time for the configured SF/BW/payload & $\pm$(SX1276 symbol-timing tolerance, typ.\ 1\,\%) \\
		$T_{\text{FPGA}}$         & \mbox{ILA} trace: UART-RX-complete $\to$ \texttt{frame\_valid} (LH3-C method) & $\pm$(1 sysclk cycle, i.e.\ 10\,ns at 100\,MHz) \\
		$T_{\text{UART\_in}}$ / $T_{\text{UART\_out}}$ & Fixed: 3.73\,ms / 3.04\,ms at 115200 baud & $\pm$(baud-divisor quantization, typ.\ 0.5\,\%) \\
		$T_{\text{infer}}$        & \texttt{time.perf\_counter()} around the \texttt{InferenceEngine.predict()} call (LH4-H microbench) & $\pm$(reported std over $\geq$100 calls) \\
		$T_{\text{RX\_FW}}$       & $T_{\text{total}}$ $-$ (all other stages above); residual & \textbf{Required:} quadrature-sum of all other stages' uncertainties \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabularx}
	\caption{Per-stage decomposition with required uncertainty bounds. Three stages are directly measured, three are calculated or inferred by subtraction. \textbf{Each row must carry an explicit uncertainty} --- instrument resolution for measured stages, tolerance or quantization for calculated stages, and the propagated residual for $T_{\text{RX\_FW}}$. Without uncertainty bounds the decomposition is not defensible as a spec claim in LH5-D.}
\end{table}

\begin{tcolorbox}[warning={Uncertainty Propagation for the Residual Stage}]
The residual term $T_{\text{RX\_FW}} = T_{\text{total}} - \sum_{i \neq \text{RX\_FW}} T_i$ inherits the uncertainty of every stage subtracted from it. Report it as
\[
\sigma_{\text{RX\_FW}} = \sqrt{\sigma_{\text{total}}^2 + \sum_{i \neq \text{RX\_FW}} \sigma_i^2}.
\]
If $\sigma_{\text{RX\_FW}}$ is comparable to the residual value itself, state clearly that the RX-firmware stage is ``not resolvable by subtraction at this instrumentation budget'' and do not present a point estimate without the bound.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Step 5 --- Alternative if no host USB-GPIO is available}
If a USB-\mbox{GPIO} adapter cannot be obtained, use the \FPGA{}'s \texttt{frame\_valid} (routed to \mbox{PMOD} JA[2] per LH3-D) as the measurement endpoint:

\[
	T_{\text{total}}^{(\text{partial})} = T_{\text{RF}} + T_{\text{RX\_FW}} + T_{\text{UART\_in}} + T_{\text{FPGA}}
\]

Then add the host-side contributions ($T_{\text{UART\_out}} + T_{\text{infer}}$) from the LH4-H microbenchmark. Document this substitution explicitly in the report --- the scope-measured envelope and the host-measured envelope are then added \emph{analytically}, not through a single end-to-end trigger. The p99 total may be slightly under-reported because this method captures the typical host latency rather than the exact tail of any given transaction.

\subsection{Step 6 --- Verification against LH3-D}
At SF7, the scope-measured $T_{\text{total}} - T_{\text{infer}} - T_{\text{UART\_out}}$ should match the LH3-D Step~4 measurement (mean near 55\,ms, std $<$5\,ms). If it does not, one of the instrumentation paths has drifted --- re-check pin assignments, trigger level, and the beacon firmware \mbox{GPIO} toggle polarity.

\begin{tcolorbox}[infobox={Why the RF Stage Dominates}]
	The \mbox{RF} on-air time is $\sim$50\,ms at SF7 and $\sim$1.5\,s at SF12, while every downstream stage is $<$10\,ms combined. The dominant stage is \emph{always} $T_{\text{RF}}$ at any LoRa SF. This has an important architectural consequence: moving the inference step onto the \FPGA{} would eliminate $T_{\text{UART\_out}} + T_{\text{infer}}$ ($\sim$8\,ms), which is a $<$15\% reduction in $T_{\text{total}}$ at SF7 and $<$1\% at SF12. The investment is only worthwhile if the host is also eliminated (standalone embedded deployment) --- a point LH5-D asks you to articulate.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Verification Checklist}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
	\item[$\square$] Beacon-side \mbox{GPIO} toggle firing on every \mbox{TX}.
	\item[$\square$] Host-side \mbox{GPIO} toggle firing on every classification (or alternative method documented).
	\item[$\square$] At least 50 $T_{\text{total}}$ measurements captured at SF7 baseline.
	\item[$\square$] Per-stage decomposition computed and checked for sum consistency ($\sum$ stages $=$ $T_{\text{total}}$).
	\item[$\square$] Cross-check against LH3-D confirms no drift in the beacon$\to$\FPGA{} envelope.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[deliverable={Lab Handout 5-C Deliverable}]
	\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.2em]
		\item Oscilloscope captures: Ch.~1 (beacon \mbox{TX}), Ch.~2 (host output), and optionally Ch.~3 (\texttt{frame\_valid}).
		\item Per-stage decomposition table at SF7 baseline (six rows).
		\item Sum-consistency check: $T_{\text{RF}} + T_{\text{RX\_FW}} + T_{\text{UART\_in}} + T_{\text{FPGA}} + T_{\text{UART\_out}} + T_{\text{infer}} \approx T_{\text{total}}$ within measurement uncertainty.
		\item Cross-check paragraph showing the LH5-C beacon$\to$\FPGA{} envelope matches the LH3-D measurement within tolerance.
	\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}
